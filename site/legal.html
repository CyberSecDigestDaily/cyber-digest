Privacy

Last updated · 21 October 2024

Cyber Digest is a UK-registered company subject to UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. We are the data controller for personal information you provide directly. Our ICO registration will be added at launch.

What we collect, and why

  • Email address — to send you the newsletter and account communications. Held in our ESP (Postmark, EU region).
  • Billing details — only for paid subscribers. Processed by Stripe (PCI-DSS Level 1); we never see card data.
  • Open / click events on emails — only as aggregate counts. We do not associate them with individual addresses.
  • Server logs — IP and user-agent, retained for 14 days, used solely for abuse mitigation.

What we don't do

  • No advertising trackers. No third-party analytics. No behavioural retargeting. No data brokers.
  • No selling, renting or sharing of personal data with third parties.
  • No AI-training use of subscriber data, ever.

Your rights

You can export, correct or delete your data at any time from your account settings, or by mailing privacy@cyberdigest.io. We respond inside ten working days.

Last updated · 21 October 2024

That's all. No analytics, no ad-tech, no consent banner — because there is nothing on this page that requires one under UK PECR.

Responsible disclosure

Last updated · 21 October 2024

If you find a vulnerability in cyberdigest.io infrastructure, please report it to security@cyberdigest.io with PGP. We acknowledge within one business day and commit to a disposition within 14 days.

We do not run a paid bounty programme but we maintain a public Hall of Thanks at /security/hall and will sign letters of recommendation for serious researchers on request.

Scope: cyberdigest.io and its subdomains, our public APIs, our email infrastructure. Out of scope: third-party services we use (Stripe, Postmark, Cloudflare), social-media impersonation, denial-of-service.
