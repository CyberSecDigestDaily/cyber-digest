About Cyber Digest

An independent intelligence layer for the people actually defending things.

We are not an aggregator. We are a small editorial desk that reads everything, triages it against what defenders need to know that day, and publishes the residue. Three sentences, every weekday, before standup.

The promise — what we are, and aren't.

Cyber Digest is a daily, independent threat-intelligence newsletter and reference site, written by practitioners for practitioners. It exists because the cybersecurity news cycle is loud, the advisory firehose is louder, and most enterprise defenders need signal — not a feed.

We are funded by reader subscriptions and a small number of vetted enterprise contracts. We take no advertising, no sponsored placement, and no paid coverage of vendors, products or research. Our editorial firewall is documented below and audited annually.

If we get something wrong, we correct it on the same URL, attach a visible correction note dated to the minute, and email subscribers whose decisions might have been affected.

Editorial standards — the rules we publish under.

The full editorial policy is reviewed annually. The summary below is the version we hold ourselves to internally; it will be ratified by an independent reader board before launch.

Sourcing

  • Two-source rule. No breach, attribution or CVE-exploitation claim is published in our voice without two independent sources. We will, with attribution, summarise a single-source claim made by a reputable vendor advisory.
  • Named vs. on-background. We prefer on-the-record sources. We will use on-background sourcing where the alternative is silence on a story of public interest.
  • No vendor briefings under embargo for stories where the vendor is itself the subject. Other embargoes are honoured to the minute.

Conflicts

  • Editorial staff hold no equity in cybersecurity vendors and may not consult for vendors covered by Cyber Digest.
  • Affected parties of any breach we cover are notified before publication and given a 24-hour window to respond.
  • We disclose every enterprise contract worth over £25,000/yr at legal.html#disclosures.

Corrections

  • Corrections live on the original URL — we do not stealth-edit. Each is dated and described.
  • Major corrections (anything that changes the underlying claim of a piece) are emailed to subscribers.
  • The current correction log is available at legal.html#corrections.

AI & generative tooling

  • We do not publish AI-generated copy in our editorial voice. We do use LLMs for first-pass triage of the advisory firehose and for translation between languages our desk does not natively cover.
  • Every byline you read corresponds to a human who has read the underlying primary sources.
