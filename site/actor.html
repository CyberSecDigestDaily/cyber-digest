Volt Typhoon

China-nexus state-sponsored operator focused on prepositioning access in US critical infrastructure. Active since at least 2021; attributed by CISA, NSA, FBI, NCSC, ASD, CCCS and Microsoft. The group's defining characteristic is restraint — long dwell, no novel malware, near-exclusive use of living-off-the-land techniques.

Targeting — sectors and geographies the group actually touches.

Confirmed targeting is concentrated on US and Pacific critical-infrastructure verticals. Observed access patterns favour OT-adjacent IT estate over the OT itself, consistent with a prepositioning-for-disruption thesis rather than data theft.

Tradecraft — what they actually do once inside.

The defining property of Volt Typhoon intrusions is what isn't there: no custom implants in long dwell windows, no commodity malware on initial endpoints, no DGAs. The group relies on a tight set of Windows administrative tools, open-source proxies (FRP, Earthworm), and stolen valid credentials harvested from public-facing edge devices.

Initial access in observed cases routinely traces to SOHO-grade and small-office edge devices — Fortinet, NetGear, Cisco SMB, ASUS routers — used both as entry and as multi-hop relay between operations. A typical chain: edge-router compromise → credential capture on an LDAP-bound jumphost → SMB lateral to a backup server → exfiltration over an already-allowed HTTPS path.
